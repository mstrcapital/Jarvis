## 2026-03-06 - Poker Trading Philosophy (Continued)

### 5. Position Sizing & Risk of Ruin (仓位管理与破产风险)
- **Concept**: Even the best hand (AA) loses ~20% of the time. Going "all-in" on every edge guarantees eventual ruin.
- **Trading Application**: High-confidence setups still fail. Professionals focus on *survival* (how much can I lose?) rather than *potential profit*.
- **Math**: A 50% loss requires a 100% gain to recover. Protect capital first.

### 6. Table Selection = Market Selection (选择战场)
- **Concept**: Being the 10th best player at a table of 9 top pros = losing. Being average at a table of amateurs = winning.
- **Trading Application**: Don't default to the hardest games (FX, Indices). Seek "soft tables" — inefficient, low-liquidity markets or prediction markets (Polymarket) where edges are larger.
- **Keywords**: "Soft tables" (软桌子), Inefficiency.

### 7. Confidence Scaling (置信度缩放)
- **Concept**: Bet size should scale with edge/win probability (like card counting).
- **Trading Application**: Most traders use static sizing. *Variable sizing* is the key: Bet big when the edge is massive; bet small or fold when the signal is weak.
- **Action**: Aggressively size up on A+ setups; reduce exposure on C-grade setups.

### 8. Process > Outcome (过程 > 结果)
- **Concept**: You can make the mathematically correct decision and still lose (variance).
- **Trading Application**: Short-term results are noise. Long-term results come from consistent, correct decisions.

- **Mental Framework**: "90% win probability but lost? No regrets. The decision was right, just bad luck." Focus on decision quality (EV+), not PnL on a single trade.
